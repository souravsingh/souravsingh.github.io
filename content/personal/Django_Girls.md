Title: Django Girls Second Edition in Pune 
Date: 16-08-2016 
Authors: Sourav Singh 
Tags: DjangoGirls,Python 

After the first successful Django Girls in Pune, I received a mail asking me to join as a coach for Django Girls Pune second edition. After checking the schedule for August, I agreed on joining as a mentor for Django Girls Pune.

The workshop was happening on Satruday,6th August. Despite the rains, I managed to reach the event venue on time. At the venue, I met Rohit(event organizer) and Swapnil(mentor). We decide to wait for a while for all the mentors and students to arrive at the venue. There were in total seven mentors and around twenty-five participants.

Till then, We start work on blowing the Django Girls letter balloons and collecting together the swag. After a tiring ordeal, We weren't able to blow all the letter balloons successfully. But we really had an awesome amount of swag for our workshop, Thanks to the sponsors! 

![Django Girls]({filename}/Images/IMG_20160806_093504.jpg)

![Django Girls]({filename}/Images/P_20160806_095311.jpg)


In my group I had five students, who came from either Computer Engineering or Electronics background and were currently studying in college. All of them were familiar with Python in some form or other, and all of them used Windows. So we start by giving a small brief info about Python, PyPI and pip for installation Python packages. Then, we move on to Python language syntax and then moved into [Django](https://www.djangoproject.com/). 

We talked about setting up the first Django Project, creating a new model for writing blog posts, Admin interface for Django and later deploy our Django code to [PythonAnywhere](https://www.pythonanywhere.com/). We weren't able to finish the [Django Girls tutorial](http://tutorial.djangogirls.org/en/) on time, but I showed the mentees a workflow for contributing to an open-source project. I also suggested that we can have an online learning session, they just have to come up with a date when they want to do so.

![Django Girls]({filename}/Images/IMG_20160806_181348.jpg)

We ended the session with a group shot and gathered together for a small Q&A session together. Students asked questions ranging from Django to contributing to open-source, and to thank these students we gave them a copy of [Tracy Osborn's](https://twitter.com/limedaring) fantastic book [Hello Web App](https://hellowebapp.com/). 

The workshop ended with a photo with all the mentors, mentees and organizer together.

![Django Girls]({filename}/Images/P_20160806_185932.jpg)

The second iteration of the workshop was really great. It was a really great time interacting with the students and the mentors and I got a "This is how a Programmer should look like" t-shirt (which you can get from [here](https://store.djangogirls.org/collections/frontpage/products/this-is-what-a-programmer-looks-like)) and a couple of Django Girls stickers. Also, Big thanks to Tracy Osborn for providing the books! Everyone really loved the book!

There will be having a workshop in [Ahmedabad](https://djangogirls.org/ahmedabad/) and [Hyderabad](https://djangogirls.org/hyderabad/) in a few days. You can find the photos of the event [here](https://drive.google.com/folderview?id=0B7Bi1E2Qh_sFRW1hcFJUOTByU0k&usp=sharing).
